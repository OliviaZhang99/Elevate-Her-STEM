Why this project matters

Students often hear about STEM careers in a very general way, but not everyone gets to hear what the path actually feels like. These interviews show the human side of STEM: curiosity, bias, setbacks, confidence, communication, family, pressure, and growth.

  • Learn how different people first became interested in STEM.
  • Read advice that is honest, practical, and student-friendly.
  • Explore how challenges and failures became part of growth.
  • Browse people by field, career area, and key themes.

About the Project

This website was created as part of a GLOCAL-supported student project. The profiles are based on survey responses from women and professionals connected to STEM. The website intentionally highlights lived experience, learning, advice, and career reflection instead of making the page feel like a promotional directory.

What we collect

We gather interview responses about career paths, early interests, setbacks, bias, achievements, skills, and advice for younger students.

How it is organized

Each profile is summarized into readable sections so students can focus on the person’s journey and lessons learned.

Why it matters

Representation can help students see that STEM paths are not always straight, perfect, or the same for everyone.

Our Mission

Our mission is to make STEM mentorship easier to access by sharing real voices from people already working, researching, leading, or building in STEM-related spaces.

Accessibility

Students can read advice without needing to cold-message professionals first.

Honesty

The project includes both achievements and difficult parts of the journey, including bias, rejection, and self-doubt.

Encouragement

The platform reminds students that there are many possible STEM paths and no single perfect timeline.

Interview Spotlights

These highlights focus on the advice, experience, and comments from the responses. Promotional links and company mentions are kept secondary so the page stays centered on mentorship.
